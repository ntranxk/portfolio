Ngoc Tran Xuan Khanh

Aspiring Software/Data Engineer | Community Leader | Young Innovator

Because I once shivered in the rain, I became the one who holds the umbrella.

My journey began with chasing medals and rankings. But true purpose found me in an unexpected moment when I stood face to face with a disabled veteran, isolated from the laughter around him. In that instant, I realized success means little if it serves only yourself. That encounter sparked my mission to build technology that uplifts the overlooked.

I developed my project, MOTIONLENS, which is a smart glasses system allowing disabled users to control devices through head movement. Along the way, I led inclusive community projects, mentored peers in coding, and embraced the power of technology as a force for equity. As a female student in STEM, I carry both the responsibility and the privilege to create meaningful change. I am driven not just to write code, but to write opportunity, building tools that give voice, dignity, and independence to those who need it most.

Career Aspirations

I aim to become a software and data engineer specializing in assistive technologies that break barriers and serve real human needs. RMIT's Software Engineering program, with its strong focus on artificial intelligence, data, and industry collaboration, offers the perfect platform to advance my skills and leadership.

Through RMIT's values of inclusion, impact, and imagination, I hope to grow into not just an engineer, but a builder of accessible solutions that create lasting, meaningful change.
